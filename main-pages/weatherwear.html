Mitigating the stressors of packing due to unpredictable weather through organisation.

Project Overview

The purpose of this project is to design an application aimed at addressing the issue of packing and selecting appropriate outfits when people are unfamiliar with the local climate of their destination.

Identifying the Issue: More often than not, packing for trips comes with stress and frustrations.

  1. Travellers are tasked with packing appropriate and sufficient clothing for travelling to various destinations. This process requires extensive research, making the process extremely time-consuming.
  2. Additionally, many travellers run into the issue of either inevitably needing to purchase more clothing once they've arrived or must now lug around excessive luggage.
  3. The temperature predicted by local weather forecasters will feel drastically different to travellers due to environmental factors such as climate zones causing humidity, dryness and other elements.
  4. This uncertainty leads to challenges when deciding what items of clothing to bring along and discomfort in clothing.

Summary of Weather Wear's Main Prototype Features

Situation of Use

The chosen situation of use focuses on helping travellers understand and be familiar with what items of clothing are appropriate to bring along during a trip with the destination's weather forecast as a guide. This will help reduce stress whilst planning and avoid unpredictable weather, leading to an overall more enjoyable journey.

Solution

With a team of 5 UX Design Students, I designed Weather Wear, which analyses users' destinations, trip duration and preferences along with real-time weather data to solve these problems.

My Role

  1. Research and investigate the paradoxes which affect the Situation of Use.
  2. Low-Fidelity Design - Wireframe, sketch and propose potential design directions for the prototype.
  3. Analyse user interview responses and implement features which address user concerns.
  4. High-Fidelity Design - Using Figma, design a prototype reflecting the user research findings and adhere to a cohesive brand identity.

Ideation Process

1. Rich Picture

In the context of this situation of use, 9 key stakeholders were identified and assigned to 1 of 3 distinct stakeholder groups. These groups are:

  1. Travellers: Both holiday and business travellers.
  2. Locals: Including friends, students and (office) workers.
  3. Implicit Stakeholders: Such as weather providers, brands, social media and companies.

2. User Research

To approach the research, our team conducted 5 interviews with 5 individuals to assess their concerns, needs and wants in the context of our situation of use.

Sampling Method: Convenience Sampling

  • Demographic: Mixed-Gender, Aged 19 - 28

Based on the conducted research, it was determined that interviewee's had major concerns in regards to packing and dressing for travel. These concerns were categorised into 4 major themes:

  1. Weather: Such as weather inaccuracy.
  2. Culture and Travel: Relating to cultural sensitivity.
  3. Social: Which looked at social media usage.
  4. Outfit-Related: Which looks at what was prioritized when choosing an outfit for the day whilst getting dressed.

3. Preliminary Prototypes

Following the identification of our Situation of Use, 5 preliminary design ideas were generated. These included different approaches and focuses, including:

  1. Weather Planning Focus - Prioritising the value of weather and numerical data.
  2. Social Sharing Focus - Emphasising social connectedness with foreigners and their outfit inspirations.
  3. Travel Planning Focus - Highlighted the organisation aspect, allowing visualisation of individual weather suitable clothing items.
  4. Business/Work-Related Focus - Aids in suggesting attire that balances work and comfort appropriate for the destination's weather.
  5. Cultural Sensitivity Focus - Designed around education for those who are particularly wary of cultural clothing customs.

Each of these focuses brought to light a different standpoint of concerns from our stakeholders.

Main Design Concept

Weather Wear is motivated by the concept that making outfit choices based solely on numerical temperature data is insufficient and leads to uncomfortable situations as it often lacks context to capture the true feel of the weather.

Instead, referring to visual references of people's outfit choices provides a more reliable and relatable guide when it comes to making appropriate outfit decisions. Thus, the prototype focuses on the travel-planning concept and the traveller stakeholder group.

Relevant Technologies

The final prototype's implemented features are inspired by 5 Relevant Technologies based on practical scenarios.

Main Prototype Development

The prototype is mainly divided into five sections: login and registration, homepage, search, luggage, personal information, and posting. For the entire prototype design, our team used colour schemes representing the four seasons: blue, green, yellow, and orange.

The inspiration for the logo came from the app's name and a staple in the wardrobe: a clothes hanger. The two "W" letters perfectly form the shape of a hanger, which not only reflects app's initials but also hints at its connection to clothing and outfits.

Sign-Up and Log-In Pages

Firstly, upon Sign-Up, users can specify any special requirements or personal conditions they are experiencing, which may affect their clothing recommendations.

Explore Pages

The user can explore their outfit suggestions based on the climate in the location of their stay. Clicking on a post and its information will show its clothing information, and if it is suitable for the user's specified requirements. The post's specific items can be added into the user's desired suitcase.

Suitcases Pages

In the Suitcases page, the user can create suitcases for a variety of locations and plan their clothing ahead of their trips. They can also pack for their dependents and tick off the items they have physically packed in their suitcases. Users can visualise their packing lists for an easier and faster viewing.

Homepage and Social Sharing Pages

These last two sections feature the homepage, which curates a tailored feed based on the user's destination and the social aspect - where the user can post their own outfits for other users to see.

User Testing - Observational Evaluation

Participants

  1. 6 Participants: Aged 19-28
  2. Convenience Sampling
  3. Targeted university students and working/non-working adults

Method

1. Think-Aloud Protocol: This form of evaluation revealed participants' confusion, preferences and expectations of the features and content in the prototype. Participants were asked to verbalise their thoughts as they interacted with the prototype; therefore, highlighting design issues such as mismatches with mental models, frustrations with efficiency and confusion over error messages.

2. Task-Based Usability Testing: This was implemented to assess the prototype design, by assigning users with specific tasks as they navigated through Weather Wear. This ensured that we were able to govern the user's actions and receive specific feedback of certain features, while still allowing them the autonomy to explore the design.

3. Open-Ended Questioning: Following each of the prescribed tasks, open-ended questioning was utilised to encourage participants to reflect on their experiences, share opinions and offer suggestions for improvements. This technique assisted in identifying design issues and misalignments with user expectations.

Evidence Collected

Based on the 3 evaluation techniques, evidence was collected through multiple channels. Firstly, participants provided verbal feedback, sharing spontaneous comments on aspects like icon visibility and functionality. Secondly, behavioural observations highlighted specific issues with certain features and screens, as well as misinterpretations of buttons and icons. Additionally, we observed users' task completion levels and noted that many tasks were more complex than expected, indicating the need for clarification on certain tasks.

Evaluation Findings: Key Points

In summary, there were 5 key recommendations for future improvements. These included;

  1. Improving navigation and usability by having clearer labels for features and streamline the dashboard for improved organisation
  2. Enhance design consistency - by adopting a more uniform layout for icons and buttons
  3. Refine functionality of features such as including filters to facilitate easier navigation
  4. Expand accessibility options and consider implementing additional functions for users with disabilities
  5. Clarify communication by removing and replacing ambiguous phrases to enhance understanding of the forecast information

Conclusion

As a result of the User Observations, I conclude that Weather Wear's design effectively addresses the problems identified in the Situation of Use through its implemented features. The Situation of Use acknowledges the time-consuming process of researching and organising clothing suitable for the travel destination's weather.

Through the design and implementation of relevant technologies, I aimed to alleviate the challenges which emerged from our Situation of Use. These included issues such as making misinformed clothing decisions due to inaccurate weather forecasts, which ultimately leads to disorganised luggage.

Additionally, I strived to address the inconvenience caused by having to purchase extra clothing due to weather-related under preparation. Enlightened by these challenges, I, along with my group-members, designed our prototype with the objective of improving the Situation of Use, rather than attempting to solve the paradox of misguided clothing decisions completely.

Consequently, the future development of Weather Wear becomes subjective and offers numerous perspectives for its progression. Based on our findings, I believe that Weather Wear can be cohesively integrated into people's daily lives and trips, for both infrequent and frequent travellers.
