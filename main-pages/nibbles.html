Making discovering and remembering new restaurants faster and easier.

Project Overview: What was behind the idea?

A casual chat with friends turned into a project. Between work colleagues, family members and university friends - a common frustration was the search for new restaurants.

Why? - Endless tabs and lists are hard to keep track of. Thus, Nibbles was formed - A food diary to save restaurants you want to visit, are planning to visit and ones you already have!

What problems would Nibbles solve?

Reduce the number of separate websites/platforms when browsing

Assist in the remembering of visited restaurants in organised categories

Facilitate the browsing of new restaurants with locational suggestions

Expedite future trips through restaurant planning and saving

My role was to...

Work with others to understand their goals and motivations when searching for new food places. Then, design a platform which expedites this process and saves the trips into meaningful categories and reviews.

With who?

Nibbles was a personal project, however, I relied heavily on those around me for constant feedback and input. This was necessary in order for Nibbles to cater to majority of users.

User Research: Product Testing & Personas

Testing Objectives

1. Determine if Nibbles would be preferred over using separate platforms to discover new restaurants

1. Identify any gaps in the system or any inconveniences in its usage or functions

Testing Process

10 Participants

4 Steps

3 Tasks

1. Initial screening - Participants were asked to complete a questionnaire regarding their experience with technology and their preferred platform and method they used to discover new restaurants

2. Low-fidelity prototype - They were then given a paper prototype of the proposed website layout in a mobile device, where they were asked to pretend to navigate through the website.

3. Tasks given - During the navigation through the prototype, participants were met with 3 scenarios which replicate situations that occur as if they were to use the website.

4. Post-testing - Once the testing was conducted, all participants completed a final questionnaire regarding their experience with the Nibbles prototype. Including - general impressions, the functionality of the features and their likelihood in recommending Nibbles to other potential users.

Trends

1. 6/10 users expressed their preference in using TikTok as their main platform to discover new restaurants

2. 5/10 participants stated that they were deterred from going to new restaurants if they were unfamiliar with the parking situation

Prototype Problems & Solutions

Problems

Potential prototype problems of Nibbles were:

1. Limited locational searching scope: The prototype only suggested restaurants within the range of the user's location.

2. Difficulty in remembering visited restaurants: There was no indication or flag to remember which restaurants the user has already visited.

3. Dietary restrictions not easily visible: A participant proposed the idea that if they were on a rush, they would love to easily identify which restaurants were vegetarian/gluten free etc.

Solutions

1. Set location availability for international too. Users can use postcodes for specific locations

2. Users can sort their saved restaurants by using a 'sort' filter by status. Statuses may be 'Planned/Have not visited/Visited'

3. Icons or a label will be provided next to each of the restaurant titles as well as the option to filter out restaurants based on dietary requirements.

Participant Feedback: What was out of scope?

Dietary Indications - As per participant problems, a label beside each restaurant indicated which dietary requirements they accommodated to (e.g., V/VG/GF) in the revised prototype.

Response - After another round of user testing, participants felt that the interface was heavily cluttered and too many pieces of information was displayed at once. As a result, I let this problem be resolved solely through the filter options.

Parking availability - As per participant problems, users stated that the availability of the parking was important knowledge for them when visiting new restaurants.

Response - As my mobile application focuses on the browsing, saving and personal reviewing of restaurants, this feature was out of my scope. Hence, I was unable to accommodate to this.

Learnings & Comments

Project Summary

Nibbles was a great project to work on and my first personal case study. Finding new ways to facilitate everyday experiences was a rewarding process, where I myself, did not even realise that the use of separate applications to perform one task was inconvenient. Overall, it was difficult to think of something new and not redundant and I would love it if Nibbles came to life one day.

What did I learn?

1. I cannot accommodate to certain aspects: One of the trends was the use of TikTok. After seeing this outcome, I aimed to incorporate TikTok into my design too; however, I realised that Nibbles was becoming an extension of TikTok, rather than its own application. Thus, I learned that it is best to design a platform that will address a variety of user needs, however, one that remains unique to its name.

2. The use of logos/branding: In my early designs, I aimed to included the logo and title of Nibbles onto every screen. However, after doing some research and viewing other and well-designed mobile applications, I realised that there is no need to include such a large title.

3. Not everything has to have a different colour: The biggest mistake I made was adding too much contrast and colour in my designs. However, again, after reviewing other applications, I then made the change to simplify my mobile application and keep the design consistent and neat.
